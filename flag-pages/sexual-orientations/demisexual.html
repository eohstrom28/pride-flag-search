Demisexual

Definiton

Disclaimer: People can experience identities in different ways. As such, people can identify with a label without exactly matching the definition because identities are fluid.

Demisexual (often shortened to "demi") is a term used by people who only experience sexual attraction after developing an emotional connection or bond with someone. The nature of the bond can vary from person to person, but possibilities can include friendship or romance. Demisexual falls under the asexual umbrella, so demisexual people might also identify as asexual. Demisexual people may experience other forms of attraction and can be any gender; the term only refers to a person's sexual orientation.

Another definition of demisexual is someone who experiences secondary sexual attraction, but not primary sexual attraction. Secondary sexual attraction develops over time and is based on the relationship and connection with a person. It is not based upon anything that is instantly available upon meeting someone, so it includes things such as personality and experiences. However, primary attraction is based upon information instantly available upon meeting someone, such as appearance.

Demisexual people can identify with another sexual orientation to describe the gender(s) of the people they eventually feel attraction to. They might even add "demi-" to the beginning of another sexual orientation label they identify with. Demisexual people might also identify with another label on the asexual spectrum, such as graysexual.
