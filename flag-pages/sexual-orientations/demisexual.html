Demisexual

Definiton

Disclaimer: People can experience identities in different ways. As such, people can identify with a label without exactly matching the definition because identities are fluid.

Demisexual (often shortened to "demi") is a term used by people who only experience sexual attraction after developing an emotional connection or bond with someone. The nature of the bond can vary from person to person, but possibilities include friendship, romance, and/or other forms or combinations of forms. Forming an emotional bond with someone creates the possibility for a demisexual person to feel sexual attraction, but does not guarantee it will occur. Demisexual falls on the asexual spectrum, so demisexual people might also identify as asexual. Demisexual people may experience other forms of attraction and can be any gender; the term only refers to a person's sexual orientation.

Another definition of demisexual is someone who experiences secondary sexual attraction, but not primary sexual attraction. Secondary sexual attraction develops over time and is based on the relationship and connection with a person. It is not based upon anything that is instantly available upon meeting someone, so it includes things such as personality and experiences. Conversely, primary attraction is based upon information that is instantly available upon meeting someone, such as appearance.

Demisexual people can identify with another sexual orientation to describe the gender(s) of the people they eventually feel attraction to. They might even add "demi-" to the beginning of another sexual orientation label they identify with. Demisexual people might also identify with another label on the asexual spectrum, such as graysexual.

Flag History and Meaning

The origin of the demisexual pride flag is disputed; some sources say it was created on The Asexual Visibility & Education Network's website around 2010 (since it is based upon the asexual flag), while others say it was uploaded to Wikimedia by the creator, Vimopu, on April 22, 2013.

Each element of the demisexual flag has its own meaning:

  • Black triangle: the wider asexual community
  • White stripe: sexuality
  • Purple stripe: community
  • Gray stripe: the wider graysexual community
