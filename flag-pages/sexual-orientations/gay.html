Gay

Definiton

Disclaimer: People can experience identities in different ways. As such, people can identify with a label without exactly matching the definition because identities are fluid.

Gay is a term used by people who experience attraction to the same gender as themselves. This page focuses on non-women, as this flag is specifically for non-women, but you can click here if you want to see the lesbian page. If you are looking for information about the rainbow pride flags, see the rainbow flag pages here.

In this context, gay is a term used by non-women who experience attraction to non-women. Gay people may or may not experience other forms of attraction and can be any gender. Gay can also be used as an umbrella term for LGBTQ+ identities.

Homosexual is another term used to describe gay people, but not as often. Many prefer the term gay instead because "homosexual" was used when being gay was considered a mental illness or disease by anti-LGBTQ+ people. Because of this, some gay people may find being called homosexual derogatory and offensive. Some gay people prefer to use gay rather than homosexual because they believe the term is too focused on physical acts instead of attraction or romance. Other queer people reject using gay to describe themselves because of the negative connotations with how the word is used in slang, undesirable cultural connotations, and/or because they find the label "gay" too restricting.

The opposite of gay is straight (heterosexual).

Flag History and Meaning

The gay men's (Vincian) pride flag was created by Tumblr user gayflagblog on July 10, 2019. A 5-stripe variant was also proposed along with it at the same time. They were created as an alternative to the original proposed gay men's pride flag. The proposed flag was created by Mod Hermy on DeviantArt on August 24, 2016 and was based on the lesbian flag. Due to many controversies, the 2019 flag was adopted in its place.

Each color of the gay men's (Vincian) flag has its own meaning:

  • Turquoise to green: community, healing, and joy (respectively)
  • White: transgender, intersex, gender non-conforming, and nonbinary people
  • Blue to purple: pure love, strength, diversity (respectively)
