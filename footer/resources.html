Resources

Crisis Hotlines

  • The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a free and confidential 24/7 hotline and online chat resource for people experiencing crisis or emotional distress.
  • The Crisis Text Line offers free and confidential 24/7 support with a trained counselor.

Support Hotlines Specifically for 2SLGBTQIA+ People

  • The Trevor Project provides free and confidential ways to reach a counselor in times of need. It also offers many guides on a variety of topics to inform about the 2SLGBTQIA+ community and related issues.
  • The LGBT National Hotline provides free and confidential support for anyone in the 2SLGBTQIA+ community. Hours vary, so be sure to check the website if you plan to use this resource.
  • The LGBT National Youth Hotline offers free and confidential support for 2SLGBTQIA+ youth. Hours vary, so make sure to check the website if you plan to use this resource.
  • The LGBT National Coming Out Support Hotline provides users with support specifically related to coming out.

Other Hotlines

  • State HIV/AIDs hotlines are available, but hours may vary depending on the state. There are also National AIDS hotlines available at (800) 342-AIDS, (800) 344-7432 (Spanish), and (800) 243-7889 (TDD).
  • The Pride Institute offers an information hotline and referrals relating to substance use disorders, sexual health issues, and mental health conditions.
  • The National Domestic Violence Hotline provides free and confidential 24/7 support, information, and referral services through call, text, and online chat services.

Other Resources

  • The "Relationship Vocabulary List" defines many terms used on this website. It's a good starting point for anyone who wants to learn more about LGBTQ+ people.
